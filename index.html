Tamales are a Mexican Christmas Eve tradition. My family makes our Tamales after Thanksgiving because making the tamales is such a long complicated process. During our tamale making parties everyone has their own job to do. One person is in charge of making the meat and putting the meat into the tamale. One person is in charge of making the masa. The next person is in charge of pressing the tamales. Lastly, is the person who wraps the tamales and places a bow on them so they stay together and then packages the dozens up to be frozen until Christmas Eve when we eat the tamales. My family has created many memories during these tamale parties. Those memories are the reason why Tamales are so special to me!
